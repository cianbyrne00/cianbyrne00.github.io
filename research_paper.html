UX Guidelines for Computer Aided Language Learning.

Click here to read this paper.

For my M.Sc. in Interactive Digital Media at Trinity College Dublin I wrote an 8000-word research paper. I was interested in the psychological connection with UX Design, so this became the basis of my research.

My paper explored how the UX/UI design of computer-aided language learning (CALL) impacts a language learner's psychology when learning English. Through a combination of primary and secondary sources, guidelines were established for future designers which concluded the paper.

The research process consisted of four sections:

  • The gathering of secondary sources and pre-existing data on the subject.
  • The creation of new data via an interview process with various participants.
  • Comparing and contrasting the prior data with the researchers' new-found data.
  • Establishing guidelines for a UX designer using all data and concluding the thesis.

The paper was awarded the grade of first class honours.

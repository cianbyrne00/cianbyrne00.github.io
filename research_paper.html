UX Guidelines for Computer Aided Language Learning

Live link to my research paper

For my M.Sc. in Interactive Digital Media at Trinity College Dublin I wrote a research paper on User Experience design principles. I was interested in the psychological impact of UX Design on a given user, so this became the basis of my research.

My paper explored how the UX/UI design of computer-aided language learning (CALL) impacts a language learner's psychology when learning English. Through an in-depth analysis and comparison of primary and secondary sources, I established UX guidelines for a language learning application.

The research process consisted of four sections:

  • The first section involved gathering and analysing secondary sources and pre-existing data on the subject.
  • The second involved creating new data via an interview process with various participants who used CALL to learn English.
  • The third section involved comparing and contrasting the secondary sources with the new-found data.
  • The final section established various guidelines for a UX designer based on the paper's findings, concluding the thesis.

The paper was awarded the grade of first class honours.
